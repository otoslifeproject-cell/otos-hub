OTOS Continuity™The case for a conversation · 02 / 06

Known to services is not the same as held between services.

OTOS Continuity™ is a non-clinical Continuity Layer for a high-cost ADHD-addiction User Journey the system already sees but does not yet hold between services. The question is narrow: can a small, governed demonstrator identify adults whose addiction may be ADHD-driven, route them into appropriate clinical review where criteria are met, and hold the consent-led Continuity Thread while existing services do their own work?

The ask

A 30-minute exploratory conversation to test whether a small, governed 50-person demonstrator is worth shaping.

Not a procurement request. No funding commitment. No clinical responsibility moves to OTOS. Just the right people in the room, deciding whether the idea is worth a next step.

The problem

The system sees this cohort constantly and pays for them in the most expensive settings it has — A&E, ICU, addiction services and mental health crisis — without ever joining up why. The founder, Dean Butler, has been on a CPFT ADHD medication-review waiting list for seven years, with no communication and no connection made between that wait, his many alcohol-related presentations and the other services involved across the system.

He suspects this is not a small edge case. Published evidence already shows ADHD is heavily over-represented in addiction cohorts, with studies placing it in a large minority of people in substance-use treatment — in some samples approaching one in three. Dean’s working hypothesis, built from his lived experience, service exposure, recovery fellowships and the evidence gathered globally, is that the repeat-relapse, alcohol cohort may sit at the sharp end of that figure. Addiction services struggle to move them forward while ADHD remains untreated, while the ADHD clinical review route itself may stall or exclude them if substance use remains active. So they keep self-medicating; referrals are made; patients are discharged with no service picking up the risk in the gaps. The teams, services and care exist. What is missing is upstream recognition, earlier intervention, stronger hand-off and continuity between them all — the infrastructure thread that could help this cohort get Out The Other Side too.

Why this matters now

The NHS Independent ADHD Taskforce (2025) put the avoidable economic cost of unsupported ADHD at least £17 billion and the NHS 10-Year Plan is explicitly pushing care upstream — hospital to community, treatment to prevention, analogue to digital. The cost keeps landing in the worst possible places. A focused conversation now could identify a practical, low-disruption next step with the potential to reduce avoidable crisis, improve outcomes and save significant money across the system.

OTOS Continuity™THE QUESTION & PROPOSAL · 03 / 06

When ADHD is left waiting, addiction does not arrive quietly — it brings chaos, crisis and cost with it.

The question is not whether the system is paying. It is whether it is paying early enough to change the outcome. The proposal is to test one question: what happens if this suspected cohort is identified earlier, ADHD is properly assessed and treatment is considered alongside addiction support instead of remaining an “ADHD only” unconnected case? The intervention question is bounded — can they be identified earlier, routed to proper assessment and supported while clinically indicated ADHD treatment is considered before years of self-medication become crisis and consequence at huge cost to service users and the entire system?

That is the mechanism OTOS is asking to test safely in others. The change for Dean was not just emotional. The recovery work, meetings, therapy, learning and support the system had already paid for began to land because the ADHD underneath was finally being addressed, supported by medication providing the executive function he had been missing all along. OTOS does not claim medication is the whole answer. It asks whether, for this cohort, earlier ADHD treatment creates the neurological opening that allows addiction recovery work to take hold.

What recovery needs, unmanaged ADHD has already taken away: planning, focus, working memory, impulse control, emotional regulation, routine, delayed reward and the capacity to learn new patterns. That is why standard recovery work can fail to land for this cohort until ADHD is recognised and treated where clinically indicated — the person is being asked to sustain the very capacities the condition is impairing. It could explain why earlier ADHD treatment may create the neurological opening for recovery work to take hold. OTOS is non-clinical: it doesn’t diagnose, prescribe or make treatment decisions — it holds the consent-led Continuity Thread while partners do.

OTOS Continuity™The enabler & what makes it different · 04 / 06

The missed flag was visible in five colours.

Identify the cohort earlier. Clinically address the driver where indicated. Hold the person long enough for support to land.

Earlier ADHD identification and treatment, where clinically indicated, is the intervention hypothesis. OTOS Continuity™ is the enabler — a non-clinical infrastructure layer that holds the person on the thread between services, supports them to wait well through existing lifestyle, living-well and addiction-support sessions informed by ADHD understanding, and delivers partner-informed micro check-ins and daily nudges that extend keyworker reach, maintain connection and generate feedback for the evidence report.

WHAT MAKES IT DIFFERENT

It is infrastructure. It is not another treatment service, not an EPR and not a clinical decision tool. It does not diagnose, prescribe, treat or replace anything. It focuses on the one thing nobody currently owns: the continuity between services and the sequencing — getting ADHD identified and treated upstream so the downstream recovery work has a better chance of landing. OTOS surfaces signals; partner teams make every clinical decision.

A patient with a documented ADHD diagnosis and waiting-list confirmation for medication review. That is not a missed flag. That is a missed flag visible in at least five different colours across multiple records, none of which were talking to each other.

OTOS Continuity™Evidence & proof · 05 / 06

Bold on pattern. Honest on proof.

Published evidence and NHS policy now point in the same direction. Three figures make the case hard to ignore: the NHS Independent ADHD Taskforce puts the avoidable economic cost of unsupported ADHD at around £17bn; the START France 2025 study reported 61.3% improvement in addictive-disorder outcomes after ADHD diagnosis and treatment in its study population; and a 2025 national-register study found ADHD medication was associated with reduced recurrent substance misuse and criminality. Dean’s case adds the lived and clinical receipt — in April 2026 an independent GMC-registered consultant psychiatrist wrote to the CPFT ADHD Team: “Adult ADHD previously complicated with excess alcohol use to self-medicate inadequately managed ADHD symptoms.” That sentence is the cohort thesis in writing, addressed to the exact team this work is being put in front of.

A fuller evidence sheet, founder chronology and £4bn cost-chain workings are available for governed follow-up review, under NDA where appropriate.

Figures shown are comparator and context evidence, not OTOS outcomes. OTOS has not yet demonstrated reduced relapse, reduced A&E attendance or savings — the proposed demonstrator is designed to test whether these become measurable.

OTOS Continuity™The demonstrator & the ask · 06 / 06

A small, governed test and a single, low-risk next step.


What could be tested

A bounded demonstrator pre-pilot build — 50 people, 12 weeks, Cambridge & Peterborough, circa £150k to cover setup, coordination, continuity support, measurement and reporting — could test the core question directly: can the learning from Dean’s journey be applied retrospectively and safely to identify others earlier, before the same pattern hardens? The intent is not to repeat his route but to use lived experience, clinical evidence and system hindsight to find and route the cohort sooner — expedite proper ADHD assessment and treatment where clinically indicated, hold people on the thread so support has a chance to land and measure how recovery outcomes change. It is an evidence budget, not a therapy budget — a surgical test, not a rollout.


What is not being asked for

At this stage this is not a request for procurement, full adoption, funding commitment or operational change, and no clinical decision-making moves to OTOS. NIA (Neurologically Informed Addiction) is proposed founder-originated terminology for “ADHD-driven addiction”, not an official diagnosis; a category-recognition / clinical-pattern review dialogue is in progress. No partner endorsement is assumed.


The meeting ask

The request is for a 30-minute exploratory conversation — to discuss the problem, test whether the cohort and the early-treatment approach are clinically meaningful, identify what would be unsafe or unworkable and decide together whether a small, governed next step is worth scoping.

"Next time we meet, I won't be your patient. I'll be your partner."

— Dean, to the alcohol liaison nurse who, on his third visit, had nowhere left to send him. Alcohol Liaison may be one of the earliest places the pattern becomes visible. In a governed test, that bedside signal could be preserved into the Continuity Thread instead of snapping at discharge.
