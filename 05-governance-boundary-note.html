Governance Boundary Note

The clinical / non-clinical line, stated once, in full, for reuse across ethics applications, protocols and partner conversations.

01

What OTOS Continuity™ is

A non-clinical Continuity Management Layer. It creates an agreed, non-clinical Continuity Thread — a visible record of the user journey made from consent, prompts, replies, silence, handoff receipts, attendance signals, drift signals and human-review events. One person, one thread, with separate service records and authorised partner views. It is not a shared clinical record and not an EPR.

02

What OTOS Continuity™ is not, and does not do

  • Does not diagnose.
  • Does not prescribe.
  • Does not manage medication.
  • Does not treat addiction.
  • Does not deliver therapy.
  • Does not provide crisis care.
  • Does not make safeguarding decisions.
  • Does not merge electronic patient records.
  • Does not automate triage.

04

Implications for ethics applications

Because OTOS does not make clinical decisions and does not alter clinical care pathways, the study risk profile is that of a non-clinical, low-risk observational and engagement-support intervention layered onto treatment as usual — not a clinical trial of a medical device or a treatment. This should be stated plainly and early in any ethics submission, and confirmed with the relevant research ethics committee rather than assumed.

05

Language to hold, consistently

  • "Treat the driver. Hold the thread. Evidence what happened." — not "OTOS treats" or "OTOS manages care."
  • "Medicine-plus-continuity", not continuity alone.
  • A shared thread, never a shared clinical record.
  • START-style figures are a real-world pathway signal, never an OTOS promise.
  • NIA is founder-proposed and submitted to RCPsych — never described as recognised or endorsed.
