Integration with NHS systems (APIs & interoperability)

GP clinical systems (IM1)

Primary-care systems such as EMIS Web and TPP SystmOne offer integration interfaces for approved third-party apps. Through NHS England's IM1 API programme, a tool can read patient information, extract data in bulk and write back to GP records. To gain access, OTOS would undergo the IM1 Pairing onboarding — completing clinical and IG prerequisites and a Solution Conformance Assessment List (SCAL) — then sign the interface licence to connect to live GP systems.

Open standards (FHIR)

The NHS uses FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) as the core standard for health-data exchange. New NHS APIs (e.g. GP Connect) are built on FHIR, so OTOS should be designed to consume and produce FHIR-formatted data for future-proof integration. This also aligns with DTAC interoperability requirements.

NHS Login & NHS App

To reach patients via the NHS App, third-party services integrate using NHS Login (secure OAuth2/OpenID Connect) and embed within the App. For example, eConsult runs inside the NHS App via a standard NHS FHIR API. OTOS would undergo NHS App partner onboarding, provide single sign-on via NHS Login, and meet technical requirements for secure data exchange.

Other mechanisms (MESH)

Where real-time API write-back isn't available, tools use MESH (Message Exchange for Social Care and Health) to send documents/extracts into NHS workflows — e.g. eConsult delivers consultation summaries into EMIS/SystmOne via MESH. OTOS should support such transfers for outcome reports or clinician alerts.

Data security & compliance frameworks

FrameworkWhat it requires
DSP Toolkit (Data Security & Protection)Annual online self-assessment mandatory for any organisation handling NHS patient data; measures compliance against the National Data Guardian's 10 standards. Required to sign an NHS data-sharing agreement.
ISO 27001International standard for Information Security Management. Highly recommended and often expected; viewed as a gold standard for protecting patient data and streamlines DSPT (overlapping controls).
DTAC (Digital Technology Assessment Criteria)NHS baseline (launched 2021) covering clinical safety, data protection, technical security, interoperability and usability/accessibility. Requires evidence such as DCB0129 clinical-safety compliance, GDPR privacy-by-design, cybersecurity (encryption, pen-testing), FHIR/open APIs and WCAG accessibility. A DTAC “pass” is an NHS stamp of approval.
Other IG expectationsUK GDPR/DPA 2018, Caldicott principles, records-management standards. Working with Probation adds MoJ data rules and safeguarding obligations. Cyber Essentials Plus is required by many public-sector contracts.

Demonstrable compliance is not just best practice but often a prerequisite for NHS partnerships — DSPT compliance is required to sign a data-sharing agreement, and commissioners increasingly ask for ISO 27001 and DTAC during procurement.

Dashboard & reporting design for KPIs

User engagement metrics

Track users enrolled, active-usage rates, session counts and duration, module-completion rates and drop-off points. Allow drill-down by cohort (probation region or NHS clinic). Example KPIs: “% of users completing at least X sessions”, “average days of use per user per month.” High engagement is linked to better outcomes, so these are closely watched.

Clinical & rehabilitative outcomes

Capture outcome measures relevant to the intervention — clinical assessments (PHQ-9, GAD-7) measured before/after, and in a probation context behavioural/social metrics (reduced re-offending, improved compliance). Link engagement to outcomes; align data collection with NICE's Evidence Standards Framework so the evidence proves effectiveness and value.

Safeguarding & risk reporting

Given vulnerable users, safeguarding is a top priority. Features should detect and act on concerns (e.g. flags for suicidal ideation). Safeguarding KPIs: number of alerts, issue types, response times/outcomes (“85% of high-risk alerts reviewed by a clinician within 1 hour”). A dedicated dashboard section with real-time alerts and an audit trail of actions taken supports both compliance and service improvement.

Reporting format & frequency

Offer a live dashboard with role-based access plus regular summary reports (monthly/quarterly) mapped to contractual KPIs. Use red/amber/green status against targets (e.g. “75% of users complete the 12-week programme”). Include benchmarking where possible, and feed into NHS England outcomes frameworks and Probation performance metrics.

Recommendations for OTOS

DO ITIntegrate with NHS clinical systems via IM1 / GP Connect. Complete IM1 pairing to connect OTOS with EMIS and SystmOne for two-way data sharing, fulfilling all clinical-safety and IG prerequisites and obtaining partner API licences.

DO ITAchieve NHS data-security compliance. Complete the DSP Toolkit (mandatory, annual) and obtain ISO 27001 certification for information-security management.

DO ITComplete a DTAC assessment. Meet all five criteria; allocate resources to produce a clinical-safety case, penetration-test results and accessibility compliance.

SHOULDUse NHS-standard APIs and identity services. Architect OTOS on FHIR-based APIs and integrate NHS Login so it can plug into the NHS App ecosystem.

SHOULDImplement comprehensive KPI dashboards. Track enrollment, active usage, completion and outcomes; surface engagement, clinical outcomes and safeguarding alerts; make it accessible to authorised NHS and Probation officials.

SHOULDAlign outcomes with NHS & Probation targets. Map reporting to commissioner KPIs — health outcomes and satisfaction for NHS England; compliance and reduced-reoffending indicators for Probation.

COULDEnhance safeguarding integration. Automatically share high-risk alerts with local NHS crisis teams or probation officers (with consent and governance).

COULDLeverage advanced analytics. Identify which engagement patterns predict best outcomes, or flag individuals likely to disengage or deteriorate, to help partners target resources.

COULDPursue broader integrations. Longer term, integrate with HMPPS case-management systems and NHS England population-health platforms (de-identified outcome data) to embed OTOS in the service landscape.
