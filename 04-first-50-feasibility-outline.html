First-50 Feasibility Study Outline

A pre-pilot feasibility design — decision-grade local evidence, not national proof, and not a rollout.

DesignSingle-site (or small cluster) feasibility study
Target n~50 participants
Outcome typeFeasibility & acceptability, not efficacy
Intended routeMRC Proof of Concept — Stage One
01

Objectives

  • Test whether a non-clinical Continuity Thread can be operated alongside a real pathway without disrupting clinical workflow.
  • Measure recruitment, consent and retention rates to inform a future pilot's sample size.
  • Assess acceptability to participants and to staff at the delivery site.
  • Establish which continuity signals (attendance, drift, handoff receipts) are feasible to capture reliably at this scale.

This study is not designed or powered to demonstrate a change in clinical outcomes. Any observed pattern in engagement is a feasibility signal, to be tested properly at pilot stage — not a claim of effect.

02

Population & setting

Adults whose unmanaged or undiagnosed ADHD has driven them into addiction treatment — often via self-medication — and who are moving between an ADHD assessment pathway and a community addiction or recovery service (e.g. CGL/CRS), at one confirmed NHS or third-sector site. This is the cohort the model was built from: people who stay inside addiction services longer than they should because the ADHD wait, not the addiction service, is the bottleneck. Recruited with the site's existing referral and consent processes. Site to be confirmed with the academic lead and named service partner — see 07 — Partner / Site Logic.

03

Method

Mixed-methods, single-arm feasibility design: quantitative continuity-signal data (consent status, prompts sent, replies, silence intervals, handoff receipts, attendance, drift flags, human-review events) alongside qualitative interviews with a subsample of participants and staff at study close.

04

Duration & indicative timeline

PhaseActivityIndicative length
Set-upEthics, site agreement, staff briefing2–3 months
Recruitment & runConsent, onboarding, live continuity thread operation4–6 months
Close & analysisQualitative interviews, data analysis, reporting2 months
05

Outputs

A feasibility report to the funder and academic lead; a go/no-go and design recommendation for a Stage Two pilot; anonymised, aggregate findings suitable for sharing with named partners and, if the academic lead agrees, for publication.

Framing, held throughout

First-50 produces decision-grade local evidence, not national proof and not a rollout. START-style engagement figures are a real-world pathway signal — never presented as an OTOS promise of outcome.

06

Limitations, stated up front

Single site, small sample, no control arm, short follow-up, and no clinical outcome measurement. These are appropriate limits for a feasibility stage and are the explicit reason a Stage Two pilot — not a national claim — is the intended next step.
