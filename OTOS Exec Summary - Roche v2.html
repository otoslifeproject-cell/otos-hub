The crisis & the opportunity

Find the cohort earlier. Test the signal. Build the evidence layer.

Thank you for asking for more. The whole idea starts with one person and one fact: the founder, Dean Butler, was treated for ADHD seven years too late — and when it finally happened, everything changed within hours. OTOS exists to test one question: what happens if this cohort is found earlier and ADHD treatment is considered where clinically indicated, instead of being left until last? For Roche, the first opportunity is identification: can a cohort that currently appears as alcohol relapse, crisis contact and treatment churn be recognised earlier through connected, consent-led signals?

This is about a specific, high-cost group the system already sees and pays for: adults whose addiction, relapse and repeat crisis may be driven by untreated or under-treated ADHD beneath the visible problem. ADHD-driven addiction is the plain-English cohort; NIA is the proposed category signal. The intervention question is bounded: can they be identified earlier, routed safely into a clinically governed priority-review / fast-track ADHD assessment and treatment route where criteria are met, and supported by continuity while clinically indicated ADHD treatment is considered earlier in the journey? The evidence to generate is measurable: does earlier clinical review plus continuity produce engagement, recovery and service-use signals in the same direction as Dean’s case and the published comparator evidence?

Clinically indicated treatment only lands if people stay connected long enough to receive it — through the long waits, referrals and discharges where they currently vanish. That is the single job of OTOS Continuity™: a non-clinical infrastructure layer that holds the person on the thread between services, so ADHD treatment — and the recovery work that follows — have a better chance of landing. Clinically governed ADHD treatment is the intervention where indicated. Continuity is the enabler. Testing the model for a defined cohort is the opportunity.

“ADHD, when unsupported, is a potent route into educational failure, long-term unemployment, crime, substance misuse, suicide, mental and physical illness. Economic costs to individuals and the government of at least £17 billion are avoidable as, when appropriately supported, people with ADHD can thrive and fully engage in a working life.”— NHS Independent ADHD Taskforce, 2025

Most of that paragraph maps directly onto Dean's story — all of it is exactly what happens when ADHD goes unsupported. The taskforce concludes that the costs are avoidable when the right support is in place. OTOS is not the right support — that is a clinical, ADHD-specialist responsibility. OTOS is what makes sure people stay connected to the right support long enough for it to land. He should never have had to do it alone. Dean recovered only by engineering the route the system should have had — creating the conditions for his own detox, securing the discharge letter, reaching the psychiatrist, restarting CGL, getting the corrected ADHD treatment, then watching the recovery work finally land. The ask in this brief is to test the model safely with a defined 50-person cohort — and learn whether the signals hold.

02
The trap

The gap between ADHD and addiction is where people get lost.

The care exists — the buildings, the teams and the funding all exist. What is missing is continuity between them, and a way out of the trap. The person is caught both ways: addiction services cannot move them forward while the ADHD is untreated, and the ADHD clinical review route stalls — or rejects them outright — while substance use is active. So they keep self-medicating. What begins as managing untreated ADHD hardens into addiction when the driver is left unresolved too long.

Meanwhile a referral is made and no one knows if it landed. A person leaves A&E into silence until the next crisis. Every broken thread is an upstream intervention point — and every missed one pushes avoidable cost back into the most expensive parts of the system. Knowing ADHD was sitting in a waiting list was the crucial information missing for the addiction side, for seven years and counting.

“I am still on the list. I repeatedly touched every service. Not one of them linked the ADHD and held me correctly.”Dean Butler, Founder — six A&E presentations, two ICU admissions, two private detoxes, dayhab, rehab & relapse.

We are already paying for this failure — just in the worst possible places. To reach the right treatment, Dean had to force the system to see the crisis clearly enough to act. He secured detox, the right discharge wording, CGL backing and private psychiatric review before ADHD treatment finally landed. That is not a moral failure; it is a design failure in how the journey is held.

The clinical anchor. RCPsych (CR235) says comorbid substance misuse “should be managed jointly between general adult and addiction services” — yet in practice it rarely happens. Dean’s own consultant framed his case not as alcoholism but its driver: he drank “to self-medicate inadequately managed ADHD symptoms.” That is the exact pattern OTOS is built around.

One person. One sentence. The whole thesis.

"Adult ADHD, previously complicated with excess alcohol use to self-medicate inadequately managed ADHD symptoms."Redacted clinical update letter · consultant psychiatrist to CPFT ADHD Team, April 2026 · full version available privately during scoping

That sentence is the cohort. Not "ADHD plus alcohol use disorder, treated separately." Not "alcohol use disorder, with ADHD as comorbidity." Adult ADHD, complicated by self-medication. The order of operations matters because it determines what the right treatment route looks like — and the order the system has run on, for decades, has been the wrong way round.

The lived experience is not the sales pitch. It is the design specification. One documented case does not prove a population — but it documents, in clinical language, that the mechanism is real in a lived user journey. A redacted version is available to partners on request as part of scoping.

How it works — and why the order matters

Continuity™ becomes a digital waiting room: not passive waiting, active holding.

OTOS occupies the space between services — where silence, waiting and lost follow-through usually sit — and turns it into a live continuity channel. It follows the person, not the silo, keeping them held until the clinical route catches up, so addiction support, when it comes, lands on a brain primed to receive it. The thread is woven from real, everyday contact: RCE ADHD waiting-well classes, active-waiting sessions and light, permission-based mobile check-ins the person opts into. Each interaction creates a secure, consent-based signal — letting the agreed partner view show who is still engaged and who is quietly slipping, without chasing, guesswork or added clinical burden. It lightens the caseload rather than adding to it.

Why the order matters — the two lists line up, item for item.

Everything addiction recovery requires, unmanaged ADHD impairs — focus, working memory, impulse control, emotional regulation, routine, delayed reward, the capacity to learn new habits at all. When the requirements of recovery and the deficits of ADHD line up that precisely, the question stops being whether they are related and becomes why we keep treating them as if they are not.

OTOS Continuity™The intervention · 07 / 10
04
Medicine-plus-Continuity

The medicine opens the door. Continuity holds it open.

If the driver is neurological, then stabilising the neurology — through proper ADHD treatment, under qualified clinical care — may be the missing prerequisite that lets recovery finally land. But medication alone is not the intervention. Medication opens the door; Continuity holds it open long enough for the person, the service and the recovery work to participate properly.

Medicine-plus-Continuity means clinical ADHD stability wrapped in adherence support, relapse-risk visibility, service continuity and behavioural reinforcement — so gains are not lost between appointments, services or motivation dips. The molecule is not the whole product. The identification, evidence and continuity route around it is.

START is a comparator signal, not a cure. After ADHD diagnosis and methylphenidate initiation, a 61.3% improvement in addictive disorders was observed in a study population. It does not prove OTOS — it supports the narrower proposition that clinically addressing the ADHD driver where indicated may make addiction recovery more usable for a defined subgroup.

Bold on pattern · honest on proof

What the published studies already show.

START · France61.3% improvement in addictive-disorder outcomes at six months when underlying ADHD was treated alongside the addiction work — one of the strongest controlled examples of treating the driver, not just the symptom.
JAMA Psychiatry30% trial-end abstinence in adults with ADHD and cocaine dependence given stimulant treatment alongside addiction support — directly contradicting the assumption that stimulants are contraindicated here. This challenges blanket assumptions and supports specialist review rather than automatic exclusion.
Kooij et al.40% of adults with ADHD show hazardous alcohol use; among adults with alcohol use disorder, 20–50% meet ADHD criteria. RCPsych CR235 settles on 34% as the median planning figure.
The system-cost case is already made — at policy level. The NHS Independent ADHD Taskforce (2025): unsupported ADHD is “a potent route into educational failure, long-term unemployment, crime, substance misuse, suicide, mental and physical illness. Economic costs… of at least £17 billion are avoidable” when people are appropriately supported.

How to read these figures. Every figure is labelled by source type — study-reported, public-figure, lived-experience, planning estimate. Study-reported figures describe research populations, not guarantees for any individual or local cohort. None of these are OTOS outcomes; they are anchors pointing the same way — strong enough to justify formal review, not to claim NIA as settled. If anything appears to overstate the evidence, correcting it is part of the scoping conversation, not separate from it.
