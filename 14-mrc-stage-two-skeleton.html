MRC Proof of Concept — Stage Two Follow-on Skeleton

A placeholder structure only. Eligibility for a Stage Two or equivalent pilot route is not yet confirmed and depends on Stage One / First-50 findings — do not draft this in earnest before that data exists.

Gate

This application should not be started until Stage One / First-50 has reported. Its content depends entirely on what that feasibility work finds — including whether it finds enough to justify a pilot at all.

Likely scaleLarger proof of concept / pilot, possibly multi-site
Depends onStage One / First-50 feasibility findings
EligibilityTo be confirmed officially — may sit with MRC, NIHR or an SBRI-style route instead
Section

What changes from Stage One

Scale (larger n, possibly multiple sites drawn from 07), and a shift from feasibility/acceptability objectives toward a properly powered test of engagement outcomes — still short of a definitive efficacy trial unless the funder route requires it.

Section

Evidence carried forward

Stage One / First-50 recruitment, retention and acceptability data; any design changes the feasibility stage recommended; explicit statement of what Stage One did not show, alongside what it did.

Section

Research questions

Framed as a pilot testing engagement/continuity outcomes at larger scale — still bounded by the non-clinical model in 05; not a claim of clinical efficacy unless co-designed with a clinical trials methodologist as such.

Section

Team changes

Confirm whether the Stage One team scales as-is or needs additional capacity (e.g. a trial statistician, additional site leads) — see 08 for the target team shape.

Section

Route to implementation

Where a positive pilot would lead next — Public Health, strategic partner, or further NIHR/Innovate UK routes — stated as a future step, not part of this application's ask.

Section

Budget & timeline

Cannot be sized meaningfully until Stage One findings and a confirmed route are in hand. Placeholder only.
