Partner / Site Logic

Why a feasibility or pilot study needs more than an academic lead and OTOS — and what each kind of site is being asked for.

02

A worked example: RCE as the "Waiting-Well" partner

RCE Wellbeing Hub already runs a course-based, CPFT-signposted offer. One credible expansion — RCE's own decision, not OTOS's — is a set of short, structured courses aimed squarely at what unmanaged ADHD erodes: routine-building, habit-stacking, and the behavioural-reinforcement science behind sustained change (the "science of sticking"). This positions RCE as active holding during the wait — a digital waiting room around their existing offer, not a passive one.

OTOS's role in that expansion stays exactly as bounded elsewhere in this pack: OTOS does not create, own or deliver course content, and does not become a clinical or therapeutic service. What OTOS Continuity™ can do is carry the consent-led reinforcement layer around content RCE already owns — a scheduled reminder for a micro-step the person has agreed to (a habit-stacking prompt, a check-in on a breathing or grounding exercise already taught in-course), and a drift signal to a human at RCE if someone goes quiet on it. See 05 for why this stays non-clinical.

Dean advises directly on this component: habit-stacking, routine and structured reinforcement are, in his own account, part of what kept him functioning through his own ADHD-driven addiction and recovery — lived-experience input, not a clinical claim.

03

Why more than one delivery site strengthens an application

A single site tests whether the model works in one context. A small number of contrasting sites — for instance one NHS mental-health pathway and one third-sector addiction or wellbeing service — tests whether the feasibility findings are an artefact of one setting or hold across different referral routes, staff models and populations. This is a standard feasibility-study strengthening move and should be sized to what the funding route and timeline allow; it is not a requirement to over-scale a pre-pilot.

04

Selection logic for a delivery site

  • An identifiable, bounded pathway with a known drop-off or discontinuity point.
  • Existing consent and referral processes the study can sit inside, rather than replace.
  • A named local sponsor or clinical lead willing to support recruitment and staff briefing.
  • Willingness to have OTOS pressure-tested as a non-clinical layer, with the boundary in 05 agreed in advance.
05

What a site is not being asked for

A site is not being asked to adopt OTOS permanently, to change its clinical protocols, to share its clinical record, or to commit beyond the study period. Site involvement is scoped to the feasibility or pilot study named in the application, with any future use a separate conversation.

Ask of a prospective site

Confirmation of interest in principle, and a named local contact — before any formal site agreement is drafted.
