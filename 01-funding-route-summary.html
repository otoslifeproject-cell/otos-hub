The ladder

OTOS Continuity™ has one evidence ladder and four funding routes sit on different rungs of it — including one, new here, that may sit before Stage One. Applying to the wrong rung at the wrong time is the main risk this pack is trying to prevent.

03

Why the fourth route matters

Dean has existing connections into the University of Cambridge. That may make an internal translational or "gap-style" route the fastest practical entry point — faster than a cold approach to a national scheme — because it can fund the unglamorous work that has to happen before any MRC or Wellcome application is competitive:

  • academic scoping and finding the right sponsor;
  • co-design and early PPI / lived-experience work;
  • feasibility planning and research protocol preparation;
  • partner and governance planning;
  • a small prototype or literature/evidence synthesis;
  • an intervention manual, data/outcome framework and health-economics scoping;
  • readying the team and paperwork for MRC Stage One or Wellcome.
04

Correct framing for this route

Dean is not asking the University to fund OTOS directly. He is trying to identify whether a Cambridge academic could lead a small internal translational / impact-acceleration application to prepare OTOS Continuity™ for a larger MRC Stage One, Wellcome, or NIHR feasibility study — with OTOS Digital Ltd as project partner throughout, on the same terms as 03 — OTOS Role as Project Partner.

Route 4 — confirmed live, not hypothetical

Cambridge holds an MRC Impact Acceleration Account (IAA) under the 2022 Harmonised IAA Scheme, and Cambridge Enterprise separately runs a £10m Technology Investment Fund (TIF, awards up to £250k) bridging early-stage research and commercialisation. Contacts: iaa@admin.cam.ac.uk (University IAA team) or enquiries@enterprise.cam.ac.uk (Cambridge Enterprise) — both published.

IAA funding explicitly excludes pure academic conferences/publications — it's for knowledge-exchange and impact work (staff time, postdoc support, partnership-building), which fits scoping and grant-readiness well. Still to confirm directly: whether OTOS Digital Ltd can be named as an external project partner on an academic-led IAA/TIF application, and the current call status.

06

What this pack does not do

It does not present OTOS Digital Ltd as an MRC lead applicant, does not present First-50 as proof of efficacy, does not treat Wellcome as a route to fund product build, and does not present the University as a source of direct OTOS funding. Each document in the pack is written to that boundary; see 05 — Governance Boundary Note and 06 — Evidence Anchors.
