# Traffic Sources & Funnel Strategy

- **Gaming Audiences:** Target traffic where gamers congregate. For example, Twitch (gaming livestreams) gets ~1.09 billion visits/month, and Discord ~715 million【53†L266-L269】. Other top gaming sites include Steam (293M), Xbox (148M), EA (115M), itch.io (106M)【53†L266-L269】. Large gamer communities also exist on Reddit (r/gaming), YouTube Gaming, major news forums (Gamespot, IGN), and Discord/Slack servers.  
- **Connecting Offers to Traffic:** Deploy affiliate offers where the audience is. E.g. run ads or sponsored streams on Twitch that link to game-related offers; post content on gaming subreddits (with compliance) linking to your blog or reviews containing affiliate links. On your own sites/blogs, write reviews or tutorials about gaming gear/software and embed affiliate links. Include sign-up forms (e.g. “Register here for a free game demo!”) that capture leads and then redirect to affiliate offers. You can also use “bridge pages” – a simple intro/opt-in page – before sending users to the affiliate link, to improve compliance and tracking.  
- **Traffic “Switching” (Funnel):** Think of it as a round-trip funnel: from an initial site (e.g. a blog post with a review) to an affiliate offer, then back to another content page. For example, a user lands on a blog review of a gaming headset (with affiliate link to buy), clicks “Try demo” (sign-up form for a trial of a related product), and then is retargeted via email or display ads back to a more detailed product guide. Each step should move the lead closer to conversion.  
- **A/B Split Testing:** Always test variants. For any landing page or ad, create two versions (A and B) with different headlines, images, or CTAs. Drive equal traffic to both and measure conversion rates. For instance, test “Free Gaming Mouse Trial” vs “Get 10% Off Gaming Mouse”. Whichever variant earns more clicks or signups, promote more heavily. Use tools like Google Optimize, Optimizely or your affiliate tracker (some allow split campaigns) to run these tests. Track final outcomes (sales or leads) to calculate which funnel yields higher ROI.   
- **Tracking Conversions:** Use unique tracking IDs/parameters on each link to see which traffic source converts. Many affiliate networks and analytics tools (Voluum, Bemob) let you attribute conversions down to the ad, keyword, or email campaign. Analyze metrics like Click-Through Rate (CTR), Conversion Rate (CR), and Cost per Acquisition (CPA) to optimize. Always attribute “gross revenue” to see if a variant increases real earnings (“GR response”).  

# Automation and Tools

- **Link Management:** Use link-cloaking or management tools (e.g. ThirstyAffiliates, Pretty Links) to handle dozens of affiliate URLs. This lets you update targets centrally. Many networks (ShareASale, CJ, Impact) provide APIs or data feeds – you can use those to automatically fetch new offers or generate deep links.  
- **Campaign Automation:** Employ marketing automation (email drip campaigns, chatbots) to deliver affiliate offers at scale. For example, capture a gamer’s email via a free guide and then use an email sequence to pitch an affiliate product.  
- **Traffic Distribution Software:** Consider affiliate “traffic managers” like AdsBridge, Binom or Funnel scripts, which can dynamically rotate visitors across different offers or landing pages based on performance. These tools can redirect traffic (“switch jockey” style) to whichever affiliate offer/conversion funnel is currently strongest.  
- **Scheduling & Scale:** Schedule social posts and ads using tools (Buffer, Ad platforms) for peak gaming hours. Use auto-bidding on ad networks to scale up top-converting funnels.  
- **Monitoring & Reporting:** Set up automated reports or dashboards (via Google Analytics + affiliate data) to continuously monitor performance. The goal is a self-optimizing system: if one offer/channel underperforms, traffic is automatically shifted to the next best.
