Cambridge Outreach — Email Drafts

Short, serious, no pressure. Each asks for the right person and twenty minutes — none send the full OTOS pack. Fill placeholders, verify the recipient's current work, and send only 02 — Academic Concept Note as attachment unless the audience column in 16 says otherwise.

1 · Senior Cambridge PI (Sahakian-style)

To[Professor Name], [Department]
SubjectA non-clinical continuity question in ADHD / addiction pathways

Dear Professor [Name],

Your work on [specific relevant work] is close to a question we've been shaping carefully rather than asserting: whether making the silence between appointments visible changes whether people stay in an ADHD or addiction pathway.

We've built a non-clinical model to test this and are looking at an MRC-style feasibility route, with an academic lead and OTOS Digital Ltd as project partner. I'd value twenty minutes to ask candidly whether this is a fit for your work, or who it might be a fit for.

No attachment beyond a one-page concept note — glad to send that ahead of a call.

With thanks, Dean [Surname], Founder, OTOS Digital Ltd

2 · Department gateway (Ford-style)

To[Department contact / office]
SubjectWho's the right person for an adult ADHD–addiction continuity study?

Dear colleagues,

I'm hoping for a steer rather than a decision. We've developed a non-clinical continuity model for adults moving between ADHD, addiction and wellbeing support, and are looking for the right academic within the department to advise on, lead, or co-lead an MRC-style feasibility study.

Could you point me to the right person, or forward the attached one-page note to whoever that might be? Happy to follow up with twenty minutes on a call.

With thanks, Dean [Surname], Founder, OTOS Digital Ltd

3 · Implementation science (THIS Institute-style)

To[Name / THIS Institute contact]
SubjectImplementation question — a non-clinical continuity layer in a real pathway

Dear [Name],

We've built a non-clinical continuity model intended to sit alongside — not replace — existing ADHD and addiction pathways, and we're at the stage of designing a proper feasibility test rather than assuming it works.

Given THIS Institute's work on implementation and real-world NHS service change, I'd value your view on whether this is a soundly designed feasibility question, and who might be best placed to lead it academically. Twenty minutes would be plenty.

With thanks, Dean [Surname], Founder, OTOS Digital Ltd

4 · Addiction psychiatry adviser (Bowden-Jones-style)

To[Name]
SubjectAddiction-interface question — non-clinical continuity model

Dear Professor [Name],

We've developed a non-clinical continuity layer aimed at the point where addiction, ADHD and recovery pathways intersect — it doesn't treat, prescribe or make clinical decisions, but tries to make the gaps between services visible.

I'd welcome your view on whether the addiction-interface framing holds up, and whether you'd be willing to advise as we shape a feasibility study, or suggest who might. Twenty minutes, no pressure.

With thanks, Dean [Surname], Founder, OTOS Digital Ltd
