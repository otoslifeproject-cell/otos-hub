Wellcome Mental Health — Research-Partner Skeleton

A research-led route into the mechanism of continuity and engagement in mental-health systems — not a route to fund product build. Confirm current Wellcome Mental Health scheme guidance before drafting.

FramingMechanism / systems research, not SaaS build funding
Lead applicantEligible academic research organisation, research-led
OTOS roleProject partner; lived-experience-originated model; technology partner
Section

Research question

Not "does OTOS work" but a genuine, open mechanism question about engagement in mental-health systems. The candidate primary question:

Among adults at the ADHD–addiction–recovery interface, what role does a visible, human-reviewed continuity signal play in sustaining engagement through the silence between appointments — and does surfacing that signal change re-engagement after drift, independent of any change to the clinical care itself?

This is answerable because the mechanism is specific, not general: unmanaged ADHD measurably impairs working memory, task initiation and delayed reward — exactly the capacities sustained engagement between contacts depends on (see the mismatch table in 02). That gives testable sub-questions Wellcome's remit is built for:

  • Does a visible continuity signal change re-engagement behaviour after silence, and if so, by what pathway — reminder, social/relational, or accountability?
  • Does the mechanism differ for ADHD-driven versus non-ADHD-driven disengagement, and what does that reveal about "waiting well" as a construct?
  • What do lived accounts of drift and re-engagement (via PPI, below) reveal about the mechanism that engagement-rate data alone would miss?

OTOS Continuity™ is the instrument that makes the mechanism observable, not the subject of the grant — the question is about engagement and continuity in mental-health systems generally, and would remain a live question even if OTOS itself did not exist.

Section

Significance for mental-health systems

Position within existing engagement/retention and implementation-science literature on mental-health and addiction pathways. The specific gap: most engagement research studies a single service in isolation; this mechanism question sits in the handoff and silence between services, where drift is least understood and least measured. Findings would speak to "waiting well" and continuity as constructs relevant well beyond ADHD/addiction — anywhere a system assumes a person can self-initiate engagement it does not actually support.

Section

Approach & methods

Likely mixed-methods, building on First-50 feasibility findings where available (see 04); qualitative work on lived experience of continuity and drop-off sits well within Wellcome's usual method range.

Section

Team & environment

Research-led team per 08, hosted at an eligible academic research organisation with a track record relevant to mental-health systems or implementation research; OTOS Digital Ltd named as project partner per 03.

Section

Patient & public involvement

Lived-experience leadership, distinct from (and in addition to) OTOS's own founder origin — Wellcome expects PPI as a research design input, not a marketing note.

Section

Ethics & governance

Non-clinical boundary statement per 05; data governance for continuity-signal data; standard Wellcome open research and data-sharing expectations.

Section

Outputs & translation

Peer-reviewed findings on the mechanism, open as far as Wellcome requires; explicit statement that translation/implementation funding (Public Health, strategic partner, Innovate UK) is a separate, later step — not part of this ask.

Framing discipline

Every section should read as a question about mental-health engagement mechanisms that happens to use OTOS as the instrument — not a case for funding OTOS's development. If a section reads like a pitch, rewrite it.
