Academic Funding Pack — 16

First Contact Pack

What to prepare and send before any academic email goes out — and the discipline of not sending the full OTOS pack cold.

The one rule

Do not send the big OTOS documents cold. First academic contact should feel serious, academic and concise — not like a business pitch or a large product pack.

01

What to send on first contact

  • A short email — see 20 — Cambridge Outreach Email Drafts.
  • One page: 02 — Academic Concept Note. This is already built and is the main first-send attachment.
  • Optionally, for a gateway / research-office / translational-funding contact only: a short version of 01 — Funding Route Summary.
02

Do not send cold

  • AZ Executive Summary (full) or Roche Executive Summary (full)
  • Angel Brief / Angel Prospectus
  • Evidence Dossier
  • Full diagram pack
  • Technical architecture
  • Operating model
  • Full First-50 build specification
  • Detailed prompt libraries
  • Event taxonomy
  • Commercial strategy
  • NDA — unless they ask for deeper material

All of the above exist elsewhere in the wider OTOS document family; none belong in a first academic email.

04

Decision checklist — before sending anything

  • Academic Concept Note (02) exists as a clean, exportable page.
  • Funding Route Summary (01) exists and now shows all four routes.
  • OTOS Role as Project Partner (03) exists.
  • A simple OTOS holding page exists, or at least a stable, professional URL — see 17 — OTOS Holding Page Brief.
  • Contact email is professional and stable.
  • No full diagrams are attached.
  • No sensitive post-NDA material is attached.
  • The NDA is ready, in case someone asks for more.
  • The outreach tracker (19) is set up.
  • The first five targets are prioritised — see 18.
  • The fourth funding route is visible in the pack as a real route, not buried under "other".
