OTOS Holding Page Brief

TODO — a credibility card, not a product launch. A one-page brief for whoever builds it (Dean, or handed to a developer).

Framing

This is a credibility card, not a product launch: something that proves OTOS Digital Ltd exists and gives an academic contact a safe, professional place to land after an email — not a marketing site.

01

Purpose

  • Show that OTOS Digital Ltd exists as a real, serious organisation.
  • Give a safe, public, non-overclaiming explanation of what OTOS Continuity™ is.
  • Give academic contacts a quick reference point after a first email.
  • Provide a professional contact route.
  • State plainly that fuller materials are available under NDA.
02

What it should include

SectionContent
IdentityOTOS Digital Ltd / OTOS Continuity™
Headline"A non-clinical continuity layer for people drifting between ADHD, addiction, recovery and wellbeing support."
The gapShort, plain-language statement of unmanaged ADHD driving self-medication into addiction, and the assessment wait that keeps people stuck there — no statistics that need defending.
The model, in plain EnglishWhat OTOS Continuity™ is, in a sentence or two, consistent with 05.
What OTOS is notNot diagnosis, not prescribing, not therapy, not crisis care, not a shared clinical record.
First-50 feasibility conceptOne sentence: a bounded, small-scale feasibility test, in planning, not yet a completed study.
Founder noteShort, personal, not a full origin story — a sentence or two on why this exists.
ContactOne professional contact email.
Closing line"Full materials available under NDA."
03

What it should not reveal

  • Full operating model
  • Full diagram set
  • Technical architecture
  • Partner strategy
  • AZ / Roche strategic logic
  • Commercial strategy
  • Prompt libraries
  • Event taxonomy
  • Detailed product screens
  • Any other post-NDA material
04

Tone & scope

One scrollable page. No navigation, no product screenshots, no pricing, no team grid beyond the founder note. Restrained, editorial, consistent with the rest of this pack — not a start-up landing page.
