Advisory board — terms & invite

A small, credible advisory board around OTOS Continuity™ — what it is, who sits on it, what members are asked for, and how to invite the next member without overstepping.

Governing principleThe board advises. It does not carry clinical responsibility, does not direct any research question, and does not make OTOS clinical. OTOS Continuity™ remains non-clinical throughout.

03

What a member is asked for

  • Occasional advice and honest challenge — not day-to-day work, not a time-heavy commitment.
  • An indicative cadence of a short call roughly every 6–8 weeks, plus ad-hoc questions by email between.
  • Willingness to be named as an adviser, where they are comfortable, to lend credibility.
  • No operational responsibility, no clinical accountability, no obligation to introduce or endorse beyond what they choose.
04

Boundaries & conflicts

  • Advisers advise; OTOS decides. Nothing about the board makes OTOS clinical or transfers clinical responsibility to a member.
  • Any member with an NHS or academic role manages conflicts of interest per their own institution's rules; OTOS discloses the advisory relationship as a matter of course.
  • Equity or other terms are agreed in writing before a seat is offered — never floated verbally as an inducement in a first conversation.
  • The board is advisory only and holds no decision-making authority over OTOS Digital Ltd.
Two-step rule

For any clinician you also want to pressure-test the model with (e.g. Dr Claire): take the honest critique first, as a critic. Only once they're warm do you raise the seat — with terms already thought through. You can only pressure-test someone once before they're inside the tent.

Dear [Name], Thank you again for [the conversation / your thoughts on OTOS Continuity™] — your read on it was exactly the kind of honest challenge it needs. As OTOS Continuity™ takes shape, I'm putting a light advisory board around it — a handful of people whose judgement I trust, to give occasional advice and, frankly, to tell me when I've got something wrong.
