The science of sticking · part one

Medication opens the learning window. OTOS keeps it open.

For the NIA cohort, recovery does not fail because the person does not care. It fails because addiction recovery asks for the exact capacities unmanaged ADHD impairs — attention, working memory, impulse control, emotional regulation, routine, delayed reward and repeated behavioural learning. That is the medical point.

CBT, relapse-prevention, recovery education, check-ins and habit-building all depend on the same neurocognitive machinery: notice the trigger, remember the strategy, pause before acting, repeat the new behaviour, and return to it tomorrow. Untreated ADHD makes that sequence fragile.

“The medication does not do the recovery work. But it creates the conditions where the recovery work can finally land.”

PublishedNICE already supports the combination

01 · NICE supports combined treatment. NICE recommends medication for adults whose ADHD symptoms remain significantly impairing, and recommends considering non-pharmacological treatment in combination with medication where adults have benefited from medication but remain impaired — which may include a full course of CBT. Medication and CBT are not competing ideas; the recognised pathway is combined.

02 · CBT lands better when the brain can use it. NICE's combination-treatment review states that medication can target core ADHD symptoms such as inattention and impulsivity, while psychosocial treatment builds longer-lasting behavioural and cognitive skills. In plain terms: medication reduces the core barrier; structured psychological work then builds the skills on top.

A treated ADHD brain can finally form and hold new pathways. The work was never wrong — the brain could not use it yet.

The science of sticking · part two

Adult trial data gives a hard clinical signal.

03 · In adult ADHD studies reviewed by NICE, medication-plus-CBT showed stronger responder outcomes than medication alone or CBT-plus-placebo in selected adult groups. These are ADHD treatment-engagement signals, not alcohol-outcome claims — they show why it is medically plausible that treating ADHD makes behavioural change more usable.

PublishedThe wider harm signal points the same way

04 · Recent large-scale evidence links ADHD medication with lower real-world harm, including reduced substance-misuse events. That does not prove OTOS. It supports the narrower, testable proposition that treating ADHD is relevant to substance-related risk — not merely to attention scores.

Stated safely: NIA does not claim ADHD medication cures alcohol addiction. It claims that, for a defined subgroup, dependence may be driven by untreated ADHD — and once the ADHD is stabilised, recovery work may become usable in a way it was not before.

The NIA mechanism

The pattern, step by step.

A simplified clinical model of how unmanaged ADHD becomes alcohol dependence — and where medication and continuity re-open the door to recovery.

Unmanaged ADHD
Low regulation, poor inhibition, under-arousal, impulsivity, emotional dysregulation.
Alcohol as regulation
Focus, relief, calm, social access and temporary baseline correction.
Dependence
Tolerance, withdrawal, reward-system capture and a deeper executive-function deficit.
Recovery mismatch
The system now asks the person to recover using attention, memory, routine and delayed reward — precisely the capacities most impaired.
Medication-supported learning window
ADHD treatment improves attention, behavioural control and the ability to follow through.
OTOS continuity layer
Micro check-ins, partner-approved next-step prompts, waiting-well learning, partner nudges and relapse-risk visibility keep the new behavioural pattern warm long enough for behaviour to stick.
The brain has to be ready to learn. The person has to stay engaged long enough for learning to become behaviour. The system has to hold the thread. That is the role of OTOS.

Claim discipline

What the evidence does — and does not — say.

The strength of the case depends on being precise about its limits. This is the discipline that protects the pilot, the partners and the people it is built for.

× What OTOS is NOT claiming
  • × That ADHD treatment automatically resolves addiction
  • × That all addiction is ADHD-driven
  • × That OTOS itself produces START-level (61.3%) outcomes
  • × That the pre-pilot will save money without local evaluation
  • × Any endorsement, certification or approval from a named body
  • × That NIA is a recognised diagnosis
✓ What the evidence DOES support
  • ✓ Treating ADHD in substance use: more benefit than risk (RCPsych)
  • ✓ Assessment appropriate in active misuse (NICE NG87)
  • ✓ Combined medication + CBT outperforms either alone (adult studies)
  • ✓ ADHD medication linked to lower real-world harm
  • ✓ A material local cohort worth a bounded, evaluated test
  • ✓ Strong alignment with NHS & DHSC policy direction
The testable proposition, stated safely

For a defined subgroup, alcohol dependence may be driven by untreated or inadequately treated ADHD. Once the ADHD is clinically stabilised, recovery work may become usable in a way it was not before. OTOS is the non-clinical continuity infrastructure designed to keep that learning window open between services — and the pre-pilot exists to decide whether to continue, continue with changes, rebuild, pause or stop.

Governance context & verification status

What the market teaches — and what is still to check.

The digital mental-health market is a cautionary tale OTOS designs against. Investment peaked at $5.1bn (2021) then plummeted; only ~1.4% of mental-health apps carry peer-reviewed evidence; the MHRA increasingly treats diagnosis / treatment-advice tools as medical devices. The lessons built in: digital tools as adjuncts, not substitutes · privacy-first, minimal data, clear consent · explicit non-clinical boundaries · evidence-led from the start.
