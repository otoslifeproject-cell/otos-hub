Academic Funding Pack — 26

Outreach sequencing plan

Who gets what, in what order — so pre-NDA academic first-contact, existing partners, and pharma never collide from the same hub before the story is settled.

The risk this plan manages

Cambridge academics, CPFT/Public Health partners, and AZ/Roche all sit behind the same working hub. Sending to all three registers at once — before the masters are signed off and before any one contact has been tested — risks two people comparing notes on different versions of the same story. Sequencing, not speed, is the safeguard.

01

Stage 0 — Internal lock (before any external send)

  • Gareth and Dean sign off the 15 core documents on the hub's task list (G/D/Ready ticks) — see the Review Wall for page-by-page sign-off.
  • The 8 Operating & Governance Masters stay in draft, post-NDA only, and are not referenced in any pre-NDA material.
  • Claim locks (§5 of the project truth file) re-checked against the Concept Note, Funding Route Summary and email drafts — no drift into euphemism.

Nothing below starts until this stage is done.

02

Stage 1 — Cover, then one narrow test

  • OTOS holding page (17) live at a stable URL — the credibility card every academic email can point to.
  • Outreach tracker (19) set up and empty, ready to log the first attempt.
  • Send to one target only from the top-5 list (18) — Professor Barbara Sahakian is the strongest topic fit — using the Concept Note (02) and nothing else.
  • Wait for a response, or 10 working days, before sending a second academic email.

The point of Stage 1 is not volume. It is finding out — cheaply, from one contact — whether the concept note, the ask, and the tone land as intended before it is repeated four more times.

03

Stage 2 — Widen the academic ring, still pre-NDA

  • On a clean or positive Stage 1 response: send to the remaining top-5 targets (18), spaced a few days apart, each with the Concept Note only.
  • Department-gateway and translational-funding contacts (Cambridge Enterprise, Psychiatry research office) can run in parallel with this — they are routing conversations, not topic-fit conversations, and carry lower drift risk.
  • Existing local contacts (CPFT, Public Health people Dean has already met) are not cold-discovery targets — treat any conversation with them as a support-letter / site-feasibility conversation, not a fresh first-contact email.

05

What stays paused until Stage 2 is proven

  • Full partner-brief sends to CPFT / CGL / RCE / Mind-WorkWell / Dr Claire-LAMP style contacts beyond the relationships Dean already holds.
  • AZ and Roche exec summaries — pharma is its own register (§7 of the project truth file) and should not be opened while the academic story is still being tested.
  • Angel Brief / Prospectus sends — no angel conversation benefits from running concurrently with an unproven academic pitch.
  • Any post-NDA material, including the eight Operating & Governance Masters, regardless of audience.
06

Decision points — where Dean needs to actively choose, not drift

  • After Stage 1's single reply (or non-reply): go / adjust the concept note / pause.
  • After Stage 2: which academic, if any, is the right Route 4 sponsor to formally ask.
  • Before Stage 3 begins: is the barrel actually clean — masters signed off, claims re-checked, tracker live — or is this still Stage 0 work in disguise.
