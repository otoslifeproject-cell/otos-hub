Research questions:

1. Does treating ADHD with stimulant medication improve addiction or substance-use outcomes?
Look for evidence on alcohol-use disorder, substance-use disorder, relapse rates, treatment retention, dropout, abstinence, reduced use, craving, recovery engagement, appointment attendance, completion of therapy / CBT / relapse-prevention work.

2. Is there specific evidence for methylphenidate?
Include immediate-release, extended-release and OROS methylphenidate. Include START study evidence and any ADHD + SUD / alcohol / cocaine / stimulant-use studies.

3. Is there specific evidence for lisdexamfetamine?
Include lisdexamfetamine, Elvanse, Vyvanse and amphetamine derivatives, especially adult ADHD with comorbid substance-use disorder or recovery-relevant outcomes.

4. Does ADHD medication improve the effectiveness of CBT or behavioural treatment?
Look for CBT plus medication vs medication alone, CBT plus medication vs CBT alone, adult ADHD responder rates, executive-function outcomes and whether medication improves capacity to engage with skills training, repetition, emotional regulation, impulse control and follow-through.

5. What does clinical guidance say?
Review NICE NG87, RCPsych CR235, international consensus statements on ADHD + SUD and any NHS, UK, European or peer-reviewed guidance on treating ADHD where substance misuse is present.

6. What are the risks and caveats?
Include stimulant misuse / diversion, long-acting formulations, monitoring, contraindications, active chaotic substance use, stabilised recovery and clinical risk management.
