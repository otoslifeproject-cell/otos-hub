## 2. Current OTOS proposition

OTOS Continuity™ is a standalone, non-clinical Continuity Layer around the existing service journey.

For Phase 1 it is designed to:

- sit independently around existing services;
- require no integration into existing clinical systems;
- remain low-burden for team leaders and keyworkers;
- enhance existing services rather than replace or manage them;
- hold non-clinical continuity between referrals, waits, discharges, silences and handoffs;
- make consented touchpoints, responses, drift, silence and handoff receipts visible to authorised users;
- leave clinical responsibility and decision-making with the relevant partner teams.

Core current language:

> The first product is not a cure. It is visibility.

> OTOS surfaces signals; partner teams make decisions. That boundary is the design, not a disclaimer.

> Known by services is not the same as held.

> The system saw what was on fire. It did not ask what was burning.

OTOS is not:

- a clinical service;
- a diagnostic or prescribing system;
- an EPR or shared clinical record;
- a clinical data pipe;
- a pathway-management platform;
- automated triage;
- a safeguarding decision system;
- a replacement for partner services;
- the owner or operator of the clinical pathway.

NIA — Neurologically Informed Addiction — remains founder-originated terminology for the ADHD-driven addiction thesis. It is not an official diagnosis or established UK commissioning term.

## 4. Phase 1 Bounded Intervention Test

Current working scope:

- 50 people;
- 12 weeks;
- Cambridge and Peterborough;
- circa £150,000;
- an evidence budget, not a therapy budget;
- a surgical test, not a rollout.

The central question is whether adults whose addiction, relapse or repeat crisis may be driven by untreated or under-treated ADHD can be:

1. recognised earlier;
2. routed properly for ADHD assessment or review;
3. treated where clinically indicated;
4. held through the gaps long enough for treatment, recovery work and behavioural change to land;
5. measured to determine whether the signal is strong enough to continue.

The intervention is not OTOS alone.

The intervention is earlier recognition, faster understanding, proper ADHD assessment and treatment where clinically indicated, plus enough continuity around the person for recovery work, handoffs and behavioural change to land.

Phase 1 is not a procurement request, full adoption, clinical service transfer or integration programme.

Older budgets of £50k–£75k, £91,520 or £100k are superseded for the current working proposition unless a later locked source explicitly changes the circa £150k envelope.

## 7. Evidence position

The current high-level evidence anchors are:

- NHS Independent ADHD Taskforce: at least £17bn in avoidable costs associated with unsupported ADHD and routes into substance misuse, crime, suicide, unemployment and wider illness;
- START France 2025: 61.3% showed improvement in addictive-disorder outcomes at six months after ADHD diagnosis and treatment;
- 2025 national-register research: ADHD medication associated with 25% reductions in recurrent substance misuse and recurrent criminality;
- wider literature indicating elevated SUD risk among people with ADHD and possible reductions in substance-related crises during treatment;
- the Science of Sticking proposition: ADHD treatment may improve the executive capacities needed to engage with, retain and repeat behavioural recovery work.

Important corrections:

- The French study is **START**, not “STRAT”.
- These are comparator/context findings, not OTOS outcomes.
- OTOS has not yet demonstrated reduced relapse, A&E attendance, criminality, service use or savings.
- The proposed test exists to find out whether the relevant signals become measurable locally.
- The £4bn language is an opportunity/cost-chain framing and must remain transparently modelled, not presented as an established OTOS saving.
- Quotes, partner claims and statistics require source-level verification before external publication.
