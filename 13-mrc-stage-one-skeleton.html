MRC Proof of Concept — Stage One Application Skeleton

StatusSkeleton only — no section drafted in full
Section

Project title

Working title, e.g. "Feasibility of a non-clinical continuity layer to support engagement in adult [ADHD / addiction] pathways." Keep it descriptive, not promotional.

Section

Lay summary

Plain-language description of the problem (unmanaged ADHD driving self-medication into addiction, an ADHD assessment wait that keeps people stuck in addiction services), what will be tested (a non-clinical continuity layer, First-50 feasibility design), and what the study will and won't tell us. Draw from 02, 21 and 06; do not overclaim.

Section

Applicant & partner details

Lead applicant institution and PI; OTOS Digital Ltd as project partner (role definition drawn from 03); named delivery site(s) drawn from 07.

Section

Background & rationale

The evidence gap on continuity and engagement in the named pathway; why a non-clinical layer is a testable, bounded intervention; reference existing (non-OTOS) literature on drop-off and re-engagement, plus the governance boundary in 05.

Section

Objectives & research questions

Feasibility and acceptability objectives only — no efficacy objective belongs at Stage One. Structure as one primary and three secondary objectives, each with a stated threshold so "feasible" is a decision, not an impression:

  • Primary: Can a non-clinical Continuity Thread be operated alongside a real ADHD/addiction pathway, at one site, without disrupting clinical workflow? Threshold: delivery-site staff confirm no disruption to existing workflow at study close.
  • Secondary — recruitment & retention: What recruitment, consent and retention rates does the cohort produce, to size a future pilot? Threshold: a specific rate range agreed with the statistician, not "high" or "acceptable" left undefined.
  • Secondary — acceptability: Is the model acceptable to participants and to staff at the delivery site? Measured via the qualitative interviews in 04, Section 03.
  • Secondary — signal feasibility: Which continuity signals (attendance, drift, handoff receipts) can be captured reliably at this scale, and which cannot?

Full framing and the "not powered for efficacy" statement in 04, Section 01 — quote it directly rather than re-deriving.

Section

Research plan & methods

Mixed-methods, single-arm feasibility design. Population: adults whose unmanaged/undiagnosed ADHD has driven them into addiction treatment, moving between an ADHD assessment pathway and a community addiction or recovery service (e.g. CGL/CRS) at one confirmed site — see 04, Section 02 for the exact cohort definition and why it was chosen. Data: quantitative continuity-signal data (consent, prompts, replies, silence intervals, handoff receipts, attendance, drift flags, human-review events) plus qualitative interviews with a participant and staff subsample at close. Sample size logic (~50) and the indicative Set-up / Recruitment & run / Close & analysis phasing are in 04, Sections 03–04 — reproduce, don't reword.

Section

Work packages & timeline

Set-up, recruitment & run, close & analysis — indicative phasing in 04, Section 04. Convert to the funder's required Gantt or milestone format.

Section

Deliverables & success criteria

Feasibility report; recruitment/consent/retention rates; acceptability findings; a go/no-go recommendation for Stage Two. Success criteria should be feasibility thresholds, not outcome thresholds.

Section

Route to Stage Two / next steps

State plainly that a positive feasibility result would support (not guarantee) a Stage Two or equivalent pilot application, subject to separate eligibility confirmation — see 14.

Section

Patient & public involvement

Named PPI / lived-experience lead (see 08); how PPI shaped study design and will shape interpretation of findings.

Section

Ethics, governance & data management

Ethics sponsor (lead institution), the non-clinical risk framing in 05, data flows and information governance for continuity-signal data.

Section

Risks & mitigations

Recruitment risk, staff time/capacity at the delivery site, data governance risk, and the risk of the study being read as a clinical trial if the boundary language slips.

Section

Budget summary

Research costs (staff time, analysis, ethics), OTOS project-partner costs (in-kind or costed, per funder rules), delivery-site costs (staff time). To be built with the lead applicant's research finance office.

Before drafting begins

Confirm the current MRC Proof of Concept Stage One call guidance, word limits and required headings directly with MRC / the lead institution's research office — this skeleton is a starting structure, not a substitute for the live call documentation.
