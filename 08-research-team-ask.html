Academic Funding Pack — 08

Research Team Ask

Who this pack still needs, stated plainly — including where a PhD student fits and where they don't.

Held clearly

A PhD student is not enough as lead on any of these routes. A PhD student is a strong contributor to literature review, co-design, qualitative work or analysis — as part of a team, under a senior PI.

01

The Principal Investigator

The ideal PI holds a substantive academic or clinical academic post: Professor, Associate Professor, Reader, Senior Lecturer, or clinical academic. Relevant fields include psychiatry, adult ADHD, addiction psychiatry, public health, digital mental health, implementation science, health services research, or clinical psychology. Neurology is a fit only where the individual specifically works on ADHD, addiction, cognition, or digital mental health — not as a general neurology credential.

02

The strongest team, in full

Principal Investigator
Professor / Associate Professor / Reader / Senior Lecturer / clinical academic — sets and holds the research question, academic credibility with the funder.
Clinical co-lead
Working clinician in the relevant pathway (ADHD, addiction, dual-diagnosis) — keeps the study grounded in real clinical practice and risk.
Implementation scientist
Designs how feasibility is actually measured and interpreted — not just whether it "worked" but why, and whether it would translate.
Health economist
Frames the cost case appropriately for a feasibility stage — sizes what a pilot or later Public Health case would need to show.
Statistician / methodologist
Sample size logic, analysis plan, and the discipline to keep First-50 honestly framed as feasibility, not efficacy.
Digital health / data governance lead
Data flows, information governance, and ethics-facing description of what OTOS captures and how it's held.
PPI / lived-experience lead
Keeps the study's design and language accountable to the people it's meant to serve — distinct from, and alongside, OTOS's own founder origin.
OTOS Digital Ltd — project partner
Intervention owner and technology partner; see 03 for the full role definition.
03

What we're asking, concretely

An introduction to, or expression of interest from, someone who could hold the PI role — or who could point us toward the right person or department. We are not asking anyone to commit to a study design before a PI is in place; the design in 04 is a starting proposal, open to a PI's revision.
