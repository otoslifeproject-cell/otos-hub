In one line

Dean built OTOS Continuity™ from his own documented experience of unmanaged ADHD, self-medicated into addiction, and the years it took to get the two treated as one problem instead of two separate ones.

02

Background

Before OTOS, Dean spent most of his adult life working in music and events, and separately built a career in mobile marketing technology — founding one of the earliest all-in-one mobile marketing platforms, working with multiple partners and channels including SMS. That platform background is not incidental: it's why OTOS Continuity™ is built the way it is — a consent-led messaging and reply layer that connects multiple separate partners around one thread, using the same SMS-and-platform thinking he'd already spent years building for other industries.

He was a high-functioning daily drinker for over thirty years before a private ADHD assessment in 2020 identified what he now understands to be the underlying driver. What followed was several more years moving between addiction services, NHS waiting lists, and a mental-health system that treated the drinking and the ADHD as unrelated — including two years of sobriety without ADHD treatment, several hospital admissions, and a period where he had to engineer his own medical crises to be taken seriously by services working to rigid entry criteria.

A privately funded psychiatric reassessment, arranged by his family, led to a clinical letter in April 2026 from an independent consultant psychiatrist to the NHS trust that had held his ADHD assessment wait — documenting ADHD complicated by alcohol use to self-medicate inadequately managed symptoms. That letter, and the years it took to get it, is the direct origin of OTOS Continuity™.

03

Why this matters for the academic conversation

Dean's role in any study is as project partner and intervention owner, not as a researcher, clinician or applicant — see 03 — OTOS Role as Project Partner. His lived experience shapes the model and is disclosed as its origin; it is never presented as evidence that the model works for anyone beyond his own case. His platform background is disclosed as the reason OTOS is a working, technically credible system rather than only an idea. See 06 — Evidence Anchors for exactly how his case is used as evidence, and its limits.

What Dean is not claiming

Not a clinician, not a researcher, not making any claim that his experience generalises. What he brings is the reason this question is worth testing properly, and a working, non-clinical model built from having lived the gap it's designed to close.
