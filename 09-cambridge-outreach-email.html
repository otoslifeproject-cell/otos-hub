Dear Professor [Name],

I'm writing because your work on [specific relevant work — e.g. implementation science in adult ADHD pathways / digital mental health engagement] sits very close to a question we've been building toward answering properly, rather than just asserting.

OTOS Continuity™ is a non-clinical layer that holds a visible thread through a care pathway — consent, prompts, replies, silence, handoff receipts, attendance and drift signals — alongside, not inside, clinical care. It doesn't diagnose, prescribe, treat or make safeguarding decisions; clinical teams keep full responsibility for those. What it's built to answer is a narrower, real question: does making the gaps between appointments visible change whether people stay in the pathway.

We think that question deserves a properly designed, independently led feasibility study — not something we mark our own homework on. We're looking at MRC Proof of Concept as the likely first route, with OTOS Digital Ltd as project partner and intervention owner, and an eligible academic or NHS research organisation as lead applicant.

I'd value thirty minutes to walk you through where this sits today and ask candidly whether it's a fit for your programme — and if it isn't, whether you could point us toward someone it might be.
