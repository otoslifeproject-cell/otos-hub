01

What has to be true before applying

  • A named Cambridge academic sponsor has agreed, in principle, to be attached to the application — IAA/TIF cannot be applied for by OTOS Digital Ltd alone.
  • That sponsor is willing for OTOS Digital Ltd to be named as an external project partner — confirm this explicitly; don't assume it.
  • A one-to-two-page case for support exists, adapted from 02 — Academic Concept Note, framed as scoping / knowledge-exchange work rather than a full feasibility study.
  • Indicative costings exist for the scoping activity itself (staff time, days, any small equipment or participant costs) — not for building or running First-50.
  • A simple work plan exists: what the funded period would actually produce (e.g. a shaped protocol, a confirmed PI, a costed Stage One application ready to submit).
  • The current call status has been confirmed directly with Cambridge Enterprise or the IAA team — rolling vs. deadline-based, typical award size, and current eligibility for a digital-health / mental-health scoping project.
02

What IAA-style funding typically covers

Knowledge-exchange and impact-preparation work: staff time, postdoc or research-assistant support, and partnership-building activity that moves a concept toward a fundable study. It explicitly excludes pure academic conferences or publications — which is why framing this as scoping/grant-readiness work (not a literature exercise) matters.

Likely to be coveredLikely not covered

03

Sequence of steps

  1. Confirm the current IAA/TIF call status and eligibility directly — see 18 for the contact route.
  2. Identify and approach a named academic sponsor (Cambridge Enterprise or the academic's own department can advise who's eligible to hold this).
  3. Confirm the sponsor is willing to name OTOS Digital Ltd as external project partner.
  4. Draft the one-to-two-page case for support together with the sponsor, from 02.
  5. Build indicative costings and a simple work plan with the sponsor's department administrator.
  6. Submit through the sponsor's institution, per Cambridge Enterprise's / IAA's own process.
To verify before use — do not assume

Exact scheme name in current use, deadlines or rolling-call status, typical award size, and whether a digital-health / mental-health service-intervention scoping project is currently eligible. None of this should be stated as fact in an outreach email until confirmed directly with iaa@admin.cam.ac.uk or enquiries@enterprise.cam.ac.uk.
