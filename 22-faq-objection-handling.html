Academic Funding Pack — 22

FAQ & Objection Handling

The questions an academic will actually ask on the first call — answered straight, not defensively. For Dean and Gareth to have in front of them, not to send.

How to use this

Keep this open during a call, not on screen. If a question isn't here after a real conversation, add it — this document should grow.

01

The model itself

"Isn't this just an app?"
No — the app or message is the smallest visible part. What's actually being tested is whether making the silence between appointments visible, and prompting a human to check in when it happens, changes whether someone stays in a pathway. The delivery mechanism (SMS, app, whatever) is incidental to that question.
"How is this different from a shared EPR or clinical record?"
An EPR holds clinical detail — diagnosis, treatment, notes. OTOS Continuity™ holds only continuity signals: was a message sent, replied to, or met with silence; did a handoff happen; was a check-in step completed. Each service keeps its own clinical record exactly as today. See 05 — Governance Boundary Note.
"Does this diagnose or treat ADHD or addiction?"
No. It doesn't diagnose, prescribe, treat, deliver therapy, provide crisis care, or make safeguarding decisions. It surfaces a signal; a person on the existing clinical or service team decides what, if anything, to do about it.
"What happens if someone's data shows they're at risk?"
A drift or silence signal is routed to a named human on the person's existing care team for review — never actioned automatically. Crisis and safeguarding pathways are completely unchanged; OTOS does not sit in that decision at all.
02

The evidence and the ask

"Isn't 'First-50' just marketing for a pilot that hasn't happened?"
First-50 hasn't run yet — that's stated plainly throughout this pack. It's a proposed feasibility design, not a completed study, and it's deliberately not powered or designed to show clinical outcomes. See 04 for what it can and can't show.
"What's the 61.3% / START figure, and can I trust it?"
It's a published, real-world engagement figure from an existing pathway, used only as a comparator — never presented as something OTOS has produced, and never as proof of what OTOS Continuity™ will achieve. Full sourcing and caveats are in 06 — Evidence Anchors.
"Why should an academic institution lead this instead of OTOS?"
Because MRC, Wellcome and NIHR routes are built for eligible research organisations, and because independent, academically led evaluation is exactly what stops this being "marked on our own homework." OTOS sits as project partner and intervention owner, not as applicant. See 03.
"What would I actually be signing up for if I said yes to a conversation?"
Nothing beyond the conversation itself. No study design is fixed yet — 04 is a starting proposal, explicitly open to a PI's revision or rejection.
03

The founder and the origin

"What's the founder's personal connection to this — and is that a conflict of interest?"
The model is founder-originated: shaped by his own documented experience of unmanaged ADHD, self-medicated into addiction, an ADHD assessment wait that kept him inside addiction services, and a private route out he had to engineer himself. That's disclosed as a matter of course, the same way any founder-led health intervention discloses its origin — it's the reason the model exists, not evidence that it works. See 02.
"Is the founder's clinical letter being used as proof?"
No — it's used once, clearly labelled, as a single case receipt that the mechanism described is real. It is never presented as generalisable evidence or as what the intervention will achieve for anyone else.
"Why RCE, CGL/CRS, CPFT specifically?"
They're the services already carrying this in Cambridgeshire and Peterborough — not chosen for convenience. Any expansion of their role (e.g. RCE's habit-stacking and routine-building courses as a "Waiting-Well" offer) is their own service decision; OTOS's role stays the consent-led continuity layer around it, not the content. See 07.
04

Money and independence

"Is OTOS a commercial company trying to get free research done?"
OTOS Digital Ltd is a company, and that's disclosed openly as a conflict of interest in any application, per standard funder rules — see 03. The ask is a properly funded, independently led feasibility study; OTOS is not asking the academic institution to fund product development.
"What does OTOS get out of this?"
Independent, credible feasibility evidence it cannot generate on its own — and, if the evidence supports it, a properly evaluated basis to take to NHS commissioners, Public Health, or later-stage funders. Nothing in the study protocol, analysis or reporting is controlled by OTOS.
