Evidence Anchors

What can be claimed today, what cannot, and why — for anyone drafting an application, protocol or outreach message.

01

Can claim

  • A working non-clinical model, with a defined feasibility design (First-50) ready to test it.
  • A founder-originated model, shaped by lived experience of pathway drop-off.
  • A clearly bounded, non-clinical role that sits alongside — not inside — clinical decision-making.
  • Real-world pathway engagement signals (e.g. START-style figures) as observed local patterns, properly sourced and dated.
02

Cannot claim

  • Efficacy, effectiveness or clinical outcome improvement — no study to date is designed or powered to show this.
  • National or generalisable proof from First-50 — it is single-site, small-sample, decision-grade local evidence only.
  • NIA as "recognised" — it is founder-proposed and submitted to RCPsych; recognition has not been granted.
  • Any wording implying OTOS treats, diagnoses, prescribes or manages care.
03

The one figure that needs most care: START / 61.3%

Where used, this figure is a real-world pathway engagement signal from a specific, named context — never presented as a controlled trial result, never presented as what OTOS itself has produced, and never presented as proof that OTOS Continuity™ improves outcomes. State the source, the context and the date every time it appears.

04

How to cite First-50 before it has run

Describe it as a planned or proposed feasibility study, not as a completed evaluation. Once it has run, describe outputs as feasibility and acceptability findings, and name the limitations (single site, small n, no control arm) in the same sentence as any positive signal.
