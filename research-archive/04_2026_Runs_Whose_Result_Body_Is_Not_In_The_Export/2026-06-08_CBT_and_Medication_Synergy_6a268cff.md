For the NIA cohort, recovery does not fail because the person does not care. It fails because addiction recovery asks for the exact capacities unmanaged ADHD impairs: attention, working memory, impulse control, emotional regulation, routine, delayed reward and repeated behavioural learning.

## WHAT THE EVIDENCE ALREADY SUPPORTS

### 01 · NICE already supports the combination  
NICE recommends medication for adults with ADHD when symptoms remain significantly impairing. It also recommends considering non-pharmacological treatment **in combination with medication** when adults have benefited from medication but remain impaired. That psychological treatment may include elements of, or a full course of, CBT.

**Plain meaning:** medication and CBT are not competing ideas. The recognised pathway is combined: medication reduces the core ADHD barrier; structured psychological work builds the skills.

### 02 · CBT lands better when the brain can use it  
NICE’s combination-treatment review states that medication can target core ADHD symptoms such as inattention and impulsivity, while psychosocial treatment can build longer-lasting behavioural and cognitive skills and strategies.

That is the OTOS position in clinical language:

**The medication does not do the recovery work. It creates the conditions where the recovery work can finally land.**

### 03 · Adult trial data gives us a hard clinical signal  
In adult ADHD studies reviewed by NICE, medication-plus-CBT showed stronger responder outcomes than medication alone or CBT-plus-placebo in selected adult groups.

Examples to use carefully:

- Medication + CBT/DBT showed **9/16 responders** versus **2/15** for medication alone in one adult study.
- Medication + CBT showed **22/41 responders** versus **9/37** for medication plus non-specific supportive therapy in another adult study.
- Stimulant + CBT showed **15/23 responders** versus **4/25** for CBT plus placebo in one adult study.

These are not alcohol-outcome claims. They are ADHD treatment-engagement signals. They show why it is medically plausible that treating ADHD can make behavioural change more usable.

### 04 · The wider harm signal points the same way  
Recent large-scale evidence links ADHD medication with lower real-world harm, including reduced substance-misuse events. That does not prove OTOS. It does support the narrower proposition that treating ADHD is relevant to substance-related risk, not merely to attention-score improvement.

## THE NIA MECHANISM

A simplified clinical model:

**Unmanaged ADHD**  
Low regulation, poor inhibition, under-arousal, impulsivity, emotional dysregulation.

**Alcohol as regulation**  
Focus, relief, calm, social access and temporary baseline correction.

**Dependence**  
Tolerance, withdrawal, reward-system capture and a deeper executive-function deficit.

**Recovery mismatch**  
The system now asks the person to recover using attention, memory, routine and delayed reward — precisely the capacities most impaired.

**Medication-supported learning window**  
ADHD treatment can improve attention, behavioural control and the ability to follow through.

**OTOS continuity layer**  
Micro check-ins, pathway reminders, waiting-well learning, partner nudges, relapse-risk visibility, recovery-literacy repetition and consent-led engagement signals keep the new pathway warm long enough for behaviour to stick.

Medication alone is not the intervention.
